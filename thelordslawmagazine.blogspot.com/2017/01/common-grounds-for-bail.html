The 2nd would one in which two opposite parties have, by chance, come across and out of fear have assaulted each other in that case both parties would be entitled bail on the assumption that they have acted in self defense. The 3rd would be one in which two opposite parties have indulged in the fight at the spur of the movement. In which case both parties may be enlarged on bail. (PLD 1977 Pesh 59)(PLJ 1977 pesh 39).
