Muhammad Jawad Hamid Vs SHO Police Station Faisal Town, Lahore

Present:          Mr. Mansoor-ur-Rehman Khan Afridi, Advocate, learned counsel for petitioner

                        Mr. Azhar Hussain Malik, learned DPP for the state.

ORDER:

            Petitioner has submitted the instant application for the direction to the respondent to register the FIR on the application for the petition.

            Arguments heard, record perused.

            At the very outset, the learned DPP has raised preliminary objections that the case FIR No. 510/14 has already been registered about the occurrence which has been narrated by the petitioner in his application submitted to the respondent and there is no lawful justification about the issuance of any direction for the registration of FIR. The learned DPP has supplemented his arguments with the case law “PLD 2010 Supreme Court 691, (2001) 6 Supreme Court Cases ISI 181, (2010) 12 Supreme Court Cases 254, 214 MLD 1033 and 2005 MLD 1114.

            Conversely, the learned counsel for the petitioner has argued that the case FIR No. 510/14 has been registered on the application of Rizwan Qadir, Hashmi, SHO, PS Faisal town, Lahore with mala fide intention in order to save actual culprits who has brutally murdered fourteen innocent persons beside injuring a large number of people through indiscriminate firing. Learned counsel for the petitioner further argued that registration of the second FIR is not only lawful but it is mandatory in the peculiar circumstances on this case. The learned counsel has supplemented his arguments with the case law “2001 SCMR 1556”, “2002 PCr LJ 247”, “PLD 2007 Lahore 370”, “1993 MLD 2059”, and “2010 Pc RLJ 89”.

            I have considered the respective contentions in the light of the principles laid down in the supra case law and have reached the conclusion that the registration of the second FIR is not barred as in the case of “Wajid Ali Khan Durrani and other Vs Government of Sindh and others (2001 SCMR 1556). August Supreme Court of Pakistan has observed that,---

            “Widows of the deceased were not satisfied with the two previously registered FIRs as they had felt that said FIRs did not reflect the true facts--- First FIR was registered at the instance of the police officer who was suspected by the widows of being accused himself in the case--- Second FIR was registered at the instance of private one of the widows who was still in police custody--- Contention of widows that two FIRs registered by the police did not transpire true facts, was no unreasonable--- Widows of the deceased filed constitution petition before the Lahore High Court seeking direction to the police concerned to record another FIR disclosing the true facts of the incident--- High Court accepted the petition and directed the police to record third FIR as prayed for by the widows holding that no hard and fast rule was that another FIR could not be registered of different versions given by the aggrieved party of the same occurrence--- Validity--- If information subsequently given to a police officer which disclosed a different offence was also cognizable by the police, then unless it was mere amplification of the first version, it must be recorded by the police”.

            Supra observation of the August Supreme Court of Pakistan reveals that if a subsequent information given to the police officer discloses different offence, which was also cognizable by the police then unless it is mere amplification of the first version, it must be recorded by the police. The complainant of case FIR 510/14 is a police officer and he has also been cited as one of the accused in the application of the petitioner.

            Perusal of the FIR No. 510/14 and the application of the petitioner reveals that the version of the petitioner is not the repetition of the contents of the supra FIR. Petitioner has leveled very serious allegations against the proposed accused that they in connivance with each other committed the murder of fourteen innocent persons and injured a huge number of people. It is further observed that the contention of learned DPP that the second FIR cannot be registered looses its substance as the local police has already recorded the second FIR 511/14, dated 08-06-2014 against one Gulu Butt who allegedly participated in the same occurrence which is subject matter of FIR No. 510/14 and the application of the petitioner.

            Learned DPP has further argued that in case FIR No. 510/14 the concerned authority has already constituted the Joint Investigation Team and the petitioner should join the investigation to record his version and if the petitioner is not satisfied with the investigation of the Joint Investigation Team, then the registration of the FIR on the application of the petitioner would be futile exercise. With regard to this contention the learned DPP, it is observed that the version contained in case FIR No. 510/14 is ex-parte version as in the application of the petitioner, which he had submitted to the SHO, Faisal Town, Lahore, through diary No. 657-5B-FI absolutely other set of allegation and facts about the disputed occurrence in which fourteen innocent persons lost their lives, and a large number were injured has been provided. The petitioner is admittedly the Director Administration of Minhaj-ul-Quran the place of occurrence and as per his version a number of activists and students belonging to Minhaj-ul-Quran have been murdered by the proposed accused, therefore, the petitioner also becomes the aggrieved person.

            In these circumstances, the respondent has not discharged his legal obligations as he should have registered the FIR on the application of the petitioner. It is further observed that in the case FIR 510/14m, it is absolutely not mentioned that whether any person had died during the occurrence or not and this fact by itself expose the worth of this FIR.

            In view of the above discussion the application in hand is hereby accepted and respondent is directed to register the FIR on the application of the petitioner and then to proceed in accordance with law in the investigation. File be consigned to the record room after its due completion and compilation.
