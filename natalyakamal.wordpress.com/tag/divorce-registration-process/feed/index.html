    Introduction:

    Muslim men have an inherent right of divorce. Muslim women only have this right if it has been delegated to them by the husband (Talaq-e-Tafweez) either at the time of marriage  (Clause 18, Nikahnama) or afterwards.

    Clause 18 of the Nikahnama reads:

    Whether the husband has delegated the power of divorce to the wife, if so, under what conditions?

    آیا شوہر نے طلاق کا حق بیوی کو تفویض کر دیا ہے اگر کر دیا ہے تو کونسی شرائط کے تحت؟   

    If a woman has been delegated this right i.e answered in the affirmative, then she will follow the same process for finalisation of divorce as the man. The relevant authority to register divorce is the concerned Arbitration Council where one files certain documents to initiate the divorce process. 

    What is the Arbitration Council:

    Arbitration Council means the Chairman of the Union Council along with representative of each party. Every geographical area comes under a Union Council.

    Documents Needed For Divorce & Subsequent Registration:

    The person executing the divorce deed has to give the divorce in writing on a stamp paper (Rs.100/), witnessed by two people and attested by Notary Public/Oath Commissioner. Afterwards you need to:

    1. Send via courier the original deed to spouse being divorced

    2. Compile documents to send to the relevant Arbitration Council ( ثالثی کونسل ) to finalise divorce and obtain divorce registration. Similar process would need to be followed after obtaining a ‘khula’ decree or in case of divorce by mutual consent, mubaraat (“مبارات”).

    Obtaining a Computerized Divorce Registration Certificate:

    Since 2016, computerised divorce registration certificate are issued by the Arbitration Council. These are often called NADRA divorce registration certificate as NADRA has implement the database called civil registration management system (CRMS). The issuing authority is still the local arbitration council. This document is vital for entering changes to your CNIC, Passport and for purposes of visas/immigration. The system is not integrated yet to automatically update your information in NADRA and you will need to take this certificate to NADRA so that your record is changed. That from 1.3.2021 the Citizen’s Facilitation Centre, G-11/4 issues the computerised divorce registration certificate (as well as Marriage Registration Certificates) subject to payment of a small fee.
